Common Herbs

Basil Mint Rosemary Parsley

Herbs Guide

Popular herbs to grow include basil, mint, rosemary, and parsley. They thrive in well-drained soil with plenty of sunlight. Regular watering and occasional fertilizing will keep them healthy. Herbs are beginner-friendly because they don’t need a large garden space; you can grow them in small pots, containers, or even windowsills. With just a little care, they can provide fresh flavors for your cooking and natural fragrances for your home.

Aside from cooking, herbs also have medicinal and practical uses. For example, mint is often used for tea and helps with digestion, while rosemary can be used for its calming aroma. Basil is perfect for pasta dishes, and parsley works as a garnish and adds extra vitamins to meals. Growing herbs at home saves money and gives you access to fresh ingredients anytime you need them.
