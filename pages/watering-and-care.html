Watering & Care Guide

Proper watering is essential for healthy plant growth. Most plants prefer deep, infrequent watering rather than shallow, frequent watering. This encourages roots to grow deeper into the soil, making plants more drought-resistant. A good rule of thumb is to water when the top inch of soil feels dry. Early morning or late afternoon is the best time to water, as it reduces evaporation and allows plants to absorb moisture before the heat of the day.

In addition to watering, regular care practices such as mulching and pruning are important. Mulching helps retain soil moisture, suppress weeds, and regulate soil temperature. Organic mulches like straw, wood chips, or compost are beneficial as they also improve soil quality as they decompose. Pruning helps remove dead or diseased parts of plants, encourages new growth, and improves air circulation, which can reduce the risk of fungal diseases. Regularly check your plants for signs of pests or diseases and take action promptly to keep your garden healthy.
