Keeping Your Plants Healthy

Proper care is essential to a successful garden. Watering, mulching, and pruning all play important roles in keeping plants strong. Overwatering can cause root rot, while underwatering leads to wilted plants. The best approach is to water when the top inch of soil feels dry.

Mulching is another way to protect your plants. By adding dried leaves, straw, or wood chips around plants, you can reduce water loss, prevent weeds, and regulate soil temperature. Mulch also enriches soil as it breaks down.

Daily observation is key. Remove dead leaves, trim overgrown parts, and check for pests. These small tasks prevent bigger problems later. With patience and attention, your plants will stay vibrant and healthy all season long.
