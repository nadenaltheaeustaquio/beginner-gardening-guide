Building Strong Foundations

Soil is the foundation of every garden. Healthy soil contains the right mix of sand, silt, clay, and organic matter. This balance ensures proper drainage while retaining enough moisture for roots. Poor soil can limit plant growth, so understanding how to improve it is essential for beginners.

Fertilizers provide additional nutrients that soil may lack. The three main nutrients plants need are nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium, often listed as N-P-K on fertilizer labels. Nitrogen helps plants produce leafy growth, phosphorus supports strong roots, and potassium improves overall health and resistance.

Organic fertilizers, such as compost and manure, are beginner-friendly because they release nutrients slowly and improve soil structure. Regularly adding organic matter ensures that your soil remains fertile over time. By investing in good soil care, you will create the best environment for your plants to thrive.
