Protecting Your Garden

One of the biggest challenges in gardening is dealing with pests and plant diseases. Small insects like aphids, whiteflies, and caterpillars can quickly damage plants by feeding on leaves. Diseases such as powdery mildew or leaf spot can also spread fast if not addressed early.

Prevention is always better than cure. Regularly inspect your plants, especially the underside of leaves, where pests often hide. Remove infected leaves immediately to stop diseases from spreading. Good spacing between plants also improves airflow, reducing fungal growth.

Many natural methods can be used for control. A spray made of mild soap and water works against aphids, while introducing beneficial insects like ladybugs helps restore balance in the garden. By staying observant and proactive, you can manage pests without relying on harmful chemicals.
